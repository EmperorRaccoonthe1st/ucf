# Moral Dilemmas

## The First Trolley Problem

    - The Driver:
        -1. Turn the lever left and kill five people.

        -2. Turn the lever right and kill a single person.

    - The Transplant:
        -1. Do nothing and let the five patients die while sparing the hermit.

        -2. Transplant the hermit's organs against his will to save five other patients.

    *The First Trolley Problem*:
        - Why is it morally permissible to run over the one in The Driver case, but morally wrong to transplant the hermit's organs in the The Transplant case?

### The Killing/Let Die Distinction
    
        - Principle A: Killing is worse than letting 5 die.
            - What does it imply about the Driver Case?
                - That it is permissible for the driver to make his decision.

        - Principle B: Killing five is morally worse than killing one.
            - What does it imply about the Surgeon Case?
                - That it is *not* permissible for the driver to make his decision.

## The Second Trolley Problem

    - The Switch:
        -1. Do nothing and let five people die.

        -2. Flip the switch and personally cause a someone to die.

    - The Footbridge:
        -1. Push the fat man over the rail murdering him and save 5 people's lives. 

        -2. Do nothing and let the trolley run over 5 people.

   *The Second Trolley Problem*: 
        - Why is it morally permissible to flip the switch in The Switch case, but morally wrong to push the fat man onto the tracks in the The Footbridge case?

    - The killing/ letting die distinction doesn't solve Second Trolley Problem. Why not?
        - Answer: In both cases, you're choosing between killing 1 and letting 5 die. Yet it's morally permissible to kill 1 in the The Switch case but not The Footbridge case.

## Threefold division of ends:

    │- 1. Instrumental:
    │   - Desirable for the sake of somthing else.
    │ 
    │- 2. Intrinsic:
    │    - Desirable for its own sake.
    │
    │- 3. Final:
    │    - Desirable for their own sake, but not desiable for the sake of anything else.

    - Moral Perfectionism:
        - The good human life if the life of full moral virtue.

## The Function Argument

    - (FA1)  The good human life is whatever life excellently fufills the characteristic human function.

    - (FA2) The characteristic human function is "activity involving reason".

    - (FA3) Therefore, the good life is a life of excellent action involving reason.

    - (FA4) The life of excellent action involving reason is the life of moral virtue. 

    - (FA5) Therefore, the good life is the life of moral virtue (FA3, FA4)

### Defense of FA1

        - Kind-Relativity (KR):
            - The good life for a K is whatever life/existence excellently fulfills the characteristic function of a K.

### Defense of FA2

        - (2.1) The characteristic human function is either biological life, or perceiving, "activity involving reason."

        - (2.2) The characteristic human function is not biological life.

        - (2.3) The characteristic human function not perceiving.

        - (2.4) Therefore, the characteristic human function is an activity involving reason.

### Defense of FA4

        - A **moral virtue** = a disposition or habit to:
            - (i) To have the right feelings (ex. emotions, desires),
                and
            - (ii) to act according to those feelings.

## Wolf on Moral Saints

    - What is a **moral saint**, according to Susan Wolf?
        - A person who every action is as morally good as possible.

    - Wolf's two kinds of moral saints:
        - 1. Loving saint
            - Personal happiness lies in the happiness of others.

        - 2. Rational Saint
            - Personal happiness does not lie in the happiness of others.

    - What are some examples of **non-moral virtues**?
        - Personal enjoyment
        - Personal Relationships
        - Knowlege

### Moral Saints Argument

        - (MS1) The moral saint's life has no non-moral virtues.

            - Defense of MS1 
                - Practice crowding out.

        - (MS2) The good life requires some non-moral virtues.

            - Defense of MS2
                - Our actual role-models have non-moral virtues.

        - (MS3) Therefore, the life of a moral saint is not a good life.

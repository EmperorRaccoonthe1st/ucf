# Final Study Guide Document Plan

## Overall Structure
The study guide will be organized into 9 major sections (Chapters 0-8). Each section will consist of:
1. **Core Concepts**: A summary of the theoretical knowledge.
2. **Key Formulas**: A quick-reference list of mathematical relationships.
3. **Problem-Solving Skills**: Step-by-step techniques derived from the ACS handout.
4. **Practice Highlights**: Specific types of questions to watch out for.

## Chapter-by-Chapter Outline

### Chapter 0: The Chemical Toolbox (Foundations)
- **Sections**: Density & Volume Displacement, Nomenclature, Classification of Matter, Metric System.
- **Key diagrams**: Particulate representations of mixtures/compounds.

### Chapter 1: Atomic Structure
- **Sections**: Subatomic Particles, Isotope Notation, Average Atomic Mass calculation.
- **Key diagrams**: Weighted average mass models.

### Chapter 2: Electronic Structure and Periodic Trends
- **Sections**: Quantum Numbers, Electron Configurations, Orbital Diagrams, Ionization Energy Trends.
- **Key diagrams**: Orbital filling diagrams (Aufbau/Hund).

### Chapter 3: Formula Calculations and the Mole
- **Sections**: Mole-Particle conversions, Empirical Formula algorithm, Percent Mass.
- **Key techniques**: "Divide by small" and assume 100g sample.

### Chapter 4: Stoichiometry
- **Sections**: Limiting Reactants, Theoretical/Percent Yield, Mass-to-Mass conversions.
- **Key techniques**: Mole ratio interpretation from balanced equations.

### Chapter 5: Solutions and Aqueous Reactions
- **Sections**: Solubility Rules, Net Ionic Equations, Oxidation States, Molarity & Dilution, Titration.
- **Key techniques**: Ion charge balancing.

### Chapter 6: Heat and Enthalpy
- **Sections**: Calorimetry ($q=mc\Delta T$), Standard Formation Equations, Reaction Energy Diagrams, Hess's Law.
- **Key diagrams**: Exothermic vs. Endothermic potential energy graphs.

### Chapter 7: Structure and Bonding
- **Sections**: Lewis Structures, Formal Charge, Lattice Energy (Coulomb's Law), VSEPR Geometry, Hybridization.
- **Key diagrams**: 3D molecular representations (wedge/dash).

### Chapter 8: States of Matter
- **Sections**: Ideal Gas Law, Kinetic Molecular Theory, Maxwell-Boltzmann Distributions, IMFs & Boiling Points.
- **Key diagrams**: Velocity distribution curves; IMFs in particulate diagrams.

## Resource Integration
- Utilize `mhchem` for all chemical formulas.
- Utilize `chemfig` for structures in Chapters 7.
- Utilize `pgfplots` for Chapter 8 Maxwell-Boltzmann curves.

# Comprehensive Topic List (Organized by Chapter)

## Chapter 0: The Chemical Toolbox (Foundations)
- **Density and Volume Displacement**: Relationship between mass, volume, and density; Archimedes' principle for volume of solids.
- **Chemical Nomenclature**: Naming ionic compounds (including variable oxidation states), polyatomic ions, binary aqueous acids, and hydrates.
- **Classification of Matter**: Elements, compounds, homogeneous and heterogeneous mixtures; particulate representations of matter.
- **Metric System and Units**: Proficiency with prefixes (nano to kilo), dimensional analysis, and scientific notation.

## Chapter 1: Atomic Structure
- **Nuclide Notation and Subatomic Particles**: Interpreting symbols for atoms/ions; calculating protons, neutrons, and electrons.
- **Average Atomic Mass**: Calculating weighted averages based on isotopic mass and abundance; visual models of isotopes.
- **Isotopes**: Definition and distinguishing characteristics compared to ions and isobars.

## Chapter 2: Electronic Structure and Periodic Trends
- **Quantum Numbers**: Rules for $n, l, m_l, m_s$ and their physical significance.
- **Electron Configurations**: Ground state (Aufbau, Pauli, Hund) and excited states; noble gas shorthand; Sn (Tin) specific configurations.
- **Orbital Diagrams**: Visual representation of sublevel filling; application of Hund's Rule to degenerate orbitals.
- **Ionization Energy**: Periodic trends (Across period vs. Down group) and identifying elements with highest/lowest IE.

## Chapter 3: Formula Calculations and the Mole
- **The Mole Concept**: Relationship between molar mass, moles, and total number of atoms/molecules.
- **Avogadro's Number**: Converting between mass, moles, and discrete particles.
- **Empirical Formula**: Calculation from percent composition by mass; multi-step arithmetic involving "divide by small."
- **Percent Mass Composition**: Calculating the contribution of an element to the total mass of a compound.

## Chapter 4: Stoichiometry
- **Limiting Reactants**: Identifying which reactant is exhausted first using stoichiometric ratios; particulate models of reactions.
- **Theoretical and Percent Yield**: Calculating maximum product possible and evaluating reaction efficiency.
- **Mass-to-Mass Stoichiometry**: Multi-step conversions between reactants and products using balanced equations.

## Chapter 5: Solutions and Aqueous Reactions
- **Precipitation and Net Ionic Equations**: Solubility rules; identifying spectator ions and solid precipitates.
- **Oxidation States**: Rules for assigning oxidation numbers; transition metal ions and polyatomic ion components.
- **Solution Concentrations (Molarity)**: Definition ($M = n/V$); ion-specific concentration calculations.
- **Dilution**: Using $M_1V_1 = M_2V_2$ and calculating volume of solvent added.
- **Titration**: Acid-base neutralization stoichiometry; determining unknown concentrations from equivalence points.

## Chapter 6: Heat and Enthalpy
- **Calorimetry and Heat Transfer**: $q = mc\Delta T$; thermal equilibrium and conservation of energy ($-q_{lost} = q_{gained}$).
- **Standard Enthalpy of Formation**: Definition; $\Delta H_f^\circ = 0$ for elements in standard states.
- **Hess's Law**: Summing reaction enthalpies; manipulating equations (reversing, scaling) to find target $\Delta H$.
- **Standard Formation Equations**: Recognizing/writing equations that form 1 mole of product from elements in standard states (using fractional coefficients).
- **Reaction Energy Diagrams**: Interpreting enthalpy vs. reaction progress graphs (identifying exothermic vs. endothermic profiles).
- **Reaction Enthalpy Calculations**: Using $\Delta H_{rxn} = \sum \text{products} - \sum \text{reactants}$.

## Chapter 7: Structure and Bonding
- **Lewis Structures and Formal Charge**: Drawing valid structures; calculating formal charge to evaluate stability.
- **Lattice Energy**: Coulomb's Law factors (ionic charge magnitude and interionic distance).
- **VSEPR Theory and Molecular Geometry**: Steric number; electron domain vs. molecular geometry; bond angle predictions.
- **Valence Bond Theory**: Sigma ($\sigma$) and Pi ($\pi$) bond counting; orbital hybridization ($sp, sp^2, sp^3$).

## Chapter 8: States of Matter
- **Ideal Gas Law**: $PV = nRT$; gas density calculations ($d = PM/RT$).
- **Kinetic Molecular Theory**: Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions; relationship between temperature, molar mass, and molecular speed.
- **Intermolecular Forces (IMFs)**: London Dispersion, Dipole-Dipole, and Hydrogen Bonding; structural requirements for H-bonding.
- **Physical Properties**: Correlation between IMF strength and boiling points; polarizability effects.
- **Visualizing IMFs**: Distinguishing between intramolecular (solid line) and intermolecular (dashed line) interactions in diagrams.

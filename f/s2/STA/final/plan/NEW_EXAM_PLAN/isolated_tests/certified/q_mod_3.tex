\section*{Question 3}
A university survey shows the following information about $250$ students:
\begin{itemize}
    \item $150$ students subscribe to Netflix
    \item $100$ students subscribe to Disney+
    \item $60$ students subscribe to both Netflix and Disney+
\end{itemize}

A student is selected at random.

\textbf{Instruction:} Show all steps. Answers without supporting work may not receive full credit.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
    \item Find the probability that the student:
    \begin{enumerate}[label=\roman*.]
        \item Subscribes to Netflix
        \item Subscribes to Disney+
        \item Subscribes to both Netflix and Disney+
    \end{enumerate}

    \item Find the probability that the student subscribes to \textbf{at least one} of the two services.

    \item Find the probability that a student subscribes to Disney+ \textbf{given that} the student subscribes to Netflix.

    \item Are the events ``subscribes to Netflix'' and ``subscribes to Disney+'' \textbf{independent}? Justify your answer using probability calculations.

    \item Suppose $3$ students are selected at random \textbf{without replacement}. Find the probability that \textbf{all three} students subscribe to Netflix.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Question 4}
A game is played where a player draws one card from a standard deck of $52$ playing cards.
\begin{itemize}
    \item If the player draws a Heart, they win $\$5$.
    \item If the player draws an Ace that is not a Heart, they win $\$10$.
    \item If the player draws any other card, they lose $\$4$.
\end{itemize}

Let $X$ be the amount of money, in dollars, won in one play of the game.

\textbf{Instruction:} Show all steps. Answers without supporting work may not receive full credit.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
    \item Construct the probability distribution of the random variable $X$.
    \item Find the expected value, $E(X)$.
    \item Find the standard deviation of $X$.
\end{enumerate}

Now suppose the game is played $12$ times independently. Let $Y$ be the number of times the player draws a Heart.

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*), resume]
    \item Find the probability that the player draws a Heart \textbf{at least 3 times}.
    \item Find the mean and standard deviation of $Y$.
\end{enumerate}
}

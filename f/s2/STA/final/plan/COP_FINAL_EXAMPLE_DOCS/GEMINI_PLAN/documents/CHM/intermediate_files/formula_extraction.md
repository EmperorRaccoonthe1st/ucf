# CHM2045 Formula and Concept Extraction

## 1. Provided on Exam

### Constants
*   **Measurements of Pressure:** $1 \text{ atm} = 760 \text{ mmHg} = 760 \text{ Torr}$
*   **Gas Constant ($R$):** $0.0821 \text{ L} \cdot \text{atm} \cdot \text{mol}^{-1} \cdot \text{K}^{-1}$
*   **Avogadro Constant ($N_A$):** $6.022 \times 10^{23} \text{ mol}^{-1}$
*   **Temperature:** $0^\circ\text{C} = 273.15 \text{ K}$

### Equations
*   **Ideal Gas Law:** $PV = nRT$
*   **Combined Gas Law:** $\frac{P_1V_1}{n_1T_1} = \frac{P_2V_2}{n_2T_2}$
*   **Graham's Law of Effusion:** $\frac{\text{rate}_1}{\text{rate}_2} = \sqrt{\frac{\text{molar mass}_2}{\text{molar mass}_1}}$
*   **Specific Heat Capacity Equation:** $q = m c_s \Delta T$

## 2. Not Provided / Must Memorize

### Equations
*   **Speed of Light / Wavelength / Frequency:** $c = \lambda \nu$
*   **Energy of a Photon (Frequency):** $E = h \nu$
*   **Energy of a Photon (Wavelength):** $E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$
*   **Rydberg Equation for Energy Change:** $\Delta E = -2.18 \times 10^{-18} \text{ J} \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$
*   **Enthalpy Change (Bond Energies):** $\Delta H^\circ = \sum (\text{bonds broken}) - \sum (\text{bonds formed})$
*   **Standard Enthalpy of Reaction:** $\Delta H^\circ = \sum n\Delta H_f^\circ(\text{products}) - \sum n\Delta H_f^\circ(\text{reactants})$
*   **Average Atomic Mass:** $\text{atomic mass} = \sum (\text{mass of the isotope})(\text{fraction abundance})$

### Solubility Guidelines for Ionic Compounds
*   **Soluble Compounds:** Compounds that contain $NO_3^-$, $C_2H_3O_2^-$, $Na^+$, $K^+$, $Li^+$, or $NH_4^+$ ions are soluble.
*   **Halides ($Cl^-$, $Br^-$, $I^-$):** Most are soluble, *except* halides of $Ag^+$, $Pb_2^+$, and $Hg_2^{2+}$.
*   **Sulfates ($SO_4^{2-}$):** Most are soluble, *except* when paired with $Ba^{2+}$, $Sr^{2+}$, $Ca^{2+}$, $Pb^{2+}$, and $Ag^+$.
*   **Sulfides ($S^{2-}$) and Hydroxides ($OH^-$):** Most are insoluble (will form a precipitate) *except* the Group 1 compounds, which are soluble. **Note:** when $S^{2-}$ pairs with $Ca^{2+}$, $Sr^{2+}$ and $Ba^{2+}$, the compounds are soluble. Also, $Ba(OH)_2$, $Ca(OH)_2$, $Sr(OH)_2$ are slightly soluble.
*   **Carbonates ($CO_3^{2-}$) and Phosphates ($PO_4^{3-}$):** Most are insoluble (will form a precipitate) *except* the Group 1 and $NH_4^+$ compounds, which are soluble.

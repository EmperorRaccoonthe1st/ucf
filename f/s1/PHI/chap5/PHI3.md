# Introduction to Philosophy
# More's Proof of an External World

## Moore's Proof

    MP1: I have two hands.

    MP2: If I have two hands, then there is an external world.

    MP3: Therefore, there is an external world.

## Moore's Reply

    Objection: Moore can't to know MP1's truth until he offers a reply to the Argument for the External World Skepticism!

    In Reply: Moore claims that the Argument for External World Skepticism is unsound. Specifically, he argues that its second premise is false.  

    MR1: If I know I have hands, then I know I'm not a BIV.

    MR2: I know I have hands.

    MR3: Therefore, I know not a BIV.

## Question Begging
    ..An argument whose premise illicitly or inappropriately assume the truth of the conclusion.
    
    Two ways to understand Moore's goal:
        - Offensive: 
            To have the goal of convincing a skeptic against their beliefs, in which it fails.

        - Defensive:
            To have the goal of protecting the belief in an external world, in which it succeeds.

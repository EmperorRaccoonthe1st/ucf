# Intro to Philosophy
# Descartes' Meditations

## First Meditation

    What is his goal?
        If one could find a stable foundation of believe out of their mind for the sciences.
    
    What does he mean by stable foundation for knowledge?
        A thing that is incapable of being doubted of which all other knowledge are rested on.
    
    Method of Belief:
        To find the one foundational belief of which all other beliefs rely on.
    
    Two kinds of beliefs:
        - Sensory Beliefs:
            Ones that depend for their justification on sense experience.
            
            Ex: Sense of body, location, kinematic

        - A priori beliefs:
            Ones that are independent of sense experience.
            
            Ex: Mathematical Truths, Logic, etc.

    Dream Hypothesis (DH):
        All of ones sensory experience are products of an extremely vivid dream.

    What beliefs does DH call into doubt?
        The belief in the reliability of ones senses.
    
    Evil Demon Hypothesis (EDH):
        I am currently being systematically deceived by an omnipotent evil demon.

    Which beliefs does EDH call into doubt?
        The certainty of anything outside of the mind.

 ## Second Meditation

    Which belief(s) survive the method of doubt in the First Meditation?
        The belief in ones self; belief that one exists.

    Descartes finds his stable foundation:
         I think therefore I am.

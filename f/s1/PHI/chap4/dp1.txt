While I agree with your evaluation on the nature of belief when relating to natural or scientific facts, I disagree with your statement that it is possible to directly control one's beliefs on subjective matters.
People are capable of acting in such a manner where their beliefs are enhanced or weakened, but that is not a direct control of beliefs in general.
They are simply performing actions that gradually either reinforce or weaken prior beliefs.
This is not choosing to directly alter a preexisting belief, it is only acting in such a way where one is altered to an extent.
This behavior cannot be described as "direct control" only just a form of control on one's belief.
When one is swayed from one belief or the other it is a reaction to stimuli rather than a conscious choice.

## Clifford's Rule (CR):
    "It is always wrong, everywhere, for anyone, to believe anything upon insufficient evidence"

### The Shipowner Case
        Version 1:
            A man sent a ship out onto a voyage after convincing himself that his ship was seaworthy upon insufficient evidence.

        Version 2:
            The ship was actually sound and it survived the voyage. The owner still developed his belief on the same evidence as he had in the prior version.
        
        What is Clifford's verdict regarding the shipowner's belief? Is it morally right or wrong?
            The shipowner's belief was morally wrong as it was founded on insufficient evidence.

        Why is the shipowner's belief morally wrong according to Clifford?
            The belief was immoral because it was created through false belief and it was socially significant.

    Socially Significant Belief:
        One that is capable of affecting the welfare of others.

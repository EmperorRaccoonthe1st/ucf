Although counter intuitive at first, there are a multitude of possible motivations for a _good and loving parent_ to allow their children to endure suffering.
In a general sense, it is the goal of loving parents to raise and nurture their children to be productive adults in society.
There exists no manner of life in this world devoid of suffering, and as a result it is in part the job of a parent to prepare their children for this reality.
Thus the motivation which would cause a parent to allow suffering onto their children arises.
Occasionally it is in the child's best interest to allow them to suffer, in order to prevent more suffering later in life, once they are without the protection of their parents.
The lessons they learn under their parent's watchful eye will guide them throughout their lifetime and be magnitudes more beneficial than their parents shielding them from the suffering.
A logical parent would exercise extreme caution when letting their children learn a lesson in this manner as there exists a thin line between causing undue damage and educating them properly.
Too great an amount of suffering would render the child damaged permanently and too minor an amount could prove ineffective.

Though one's first reaction might be to expel all pain, there exists a deeper relationship between well-being and threats to it.

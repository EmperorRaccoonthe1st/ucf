# Is Morality Relative?

## Proposition

    A proposition can be true or false in two different ways:
        - _Relatively_: True/False only relative to a given human belief or practice.
            - ex: I like vanilla ice cream.

        - _Objectively_: True/False independently of human belief/practice.
            - ex: The earth orbits the sun.

## Cultural Relativism

    - What is a moral proposition?
        - A value statement based on moral values.

    - **Moral Relativism**
        - Moral propositions are only relatively true/false

        -1. _Individual Relativism_ (IR) = moral propositions are true or false only relative to a given individual's moral beliefs and moral practices.

        -2. _Cultural Relativism_ (CR) = Moral propositions are true/false only relative to a given culture's belief's/practices.

    - Moral Objectivism:
        - Moral propositions are objectively true/false.

## The Cultural Differences Argument

- What are _Cultural Differences_ regarding morality?
    - Moral beliefs and practices can vary dramatically across cultures, leading to different views on what is considered right or wrong.
        
        - ex: Inuit infanticide and American anti-abortion advocates.

    - (CD1) There are cultural differences regarding moral propositions.
    - (CD2) Any propositions about which there are cultural differences is true/false relative to culture.
    - (CD3) Therefore, moral propositions are only true/false relative to a culture's beliefs or practices.

### Rachel's Objection to CD1

        - Two aspects to culture's moral system:
            - (i) _Moral Code_: which specific behaviors and practices (ex: infanticide, meat eating polygamy, etc.) are permitted, prohibited, or required.
            
            - (ii) _Moral Values_: general principles about what is intrinsically good or valuable.
        
        - A culture's moral code is determined by its moral values plus a unique set of environmental circumstances, scientific assumptions, and religious beliefs.

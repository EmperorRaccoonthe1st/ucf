# Moral Theories

## Contsequentialism

    Contsequentialism: 
        The moral value from an action is derived from the outcome resulting from it.
        An action is morally right if and only if it produces the best consequences compared to anything else you could do in the circumstances.

    Hedonistic Theory of Value:
       The only intrinsic good is happiness and the only intrinsic bad is unhappiness. 

    Combine to make -> Utilitarianism:
        - An action is morally right if and only if it produces maximal net happiness.

## Kantianism

    Human beings, unlike mere things, have reason. They are thus "ends-in-themselves."

    Two kinds of imperatives:

    - Hypothetical Imperatives: 
        Says one ought to do something if one wants to "achieve something else"
        
        Ex: "You should turn left if you want to get to the stadium"

## Moral Dilemmas (Trolleys and the like....)

    - According to Kantianism, is it morally permissible to flip the switch?
        Well it's complicated....
        It depends if the man tied to the rail is being used as as means, does the choice violate this rights and consciousness? 

    - According to Kantianism, is it morally permissible to push the fat man?
        No, you are using the Fat Man as a means not as an end.

    - According to Contsequentialism, is it morally permissible to flip the switch?
        Yes, as flipping the switch would limit the total amount of death, one instead of five, flipping the switch would then maximize net utility, therefore resulting in the best net consequences.
        
    - According to Contsequentialism, is it morally permissible to push the fat man?
        Yes, as with the previous situation the total utility would still be maximized as one would die instead of five.

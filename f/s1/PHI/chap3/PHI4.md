# Is abortion Immoral?

## The Right to Life Argument

    Fetal Personhood Question: When does a fetus become a person?

    RL1: Every fetus is a person with a right to life.

    RL2: It is morally wrong to kill a person with a right to life.

    RL3: Therefore, it is morally wrong to kill a fetus.

    Which premise does Thomson concede (for the sake of argument)?
       That the fetus is a person with the right to life; RL1. 

    Which premise does Thomson claim as false?
       RL2 

## Violinist Thought Experiment

    Version 1: He needs your kidneys for 9 months.

    Version 2: You will die if you stay plugged in.

    The violinist thought experiment has three functions:
        1. An apparent counterexample to RL2

        2. The basis of an argument of abortion's permissibility.

        3. A device for clarifying the concept for right to life.

## Thomson's Argument from Analogy

    Thomson offers an argument from analogy for the moral permissibility of abortion.

    V1: It is not morally wrong to unplug yourself from the violinist.

    V2: If it is not morally wrong to unplug yourself from the violinist, then it is not morally wrong to abort a fetus.

    V3: Therefore, it is not morally wrong to abort a fetus.

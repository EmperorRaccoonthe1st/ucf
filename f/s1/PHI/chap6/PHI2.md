The Threat of Determination

## Incompatibility and the Consequence Argument

    Incompatibilism:
       If determinism is true, then we have no free will.

    Unalterable Fact:
         A fact that no human being in history that no human being could ever have the ability to change.

        Ex: Mathematical Truths, Natural Laws

    The Unalterably Principle:
        If its an unalterable fact that p and its an unalterable fact that q is a necessary consequence of p, then its an unalterable fact that q.


# The Consequence Argument

    C1: Determinism is true

    C2: Its an unalterable fact that the state of the world long before humans and the laws of nature are unalterable facts.

    C3: Its an unalterable fact that that state of the world long before humans and the natural laws necessitate that I'm taking PHI 2010.

    C4: Therefore, its an unalterable fact that I'm taking PHI 2010. (C2, C3)

    C5: Therefore, I'm not taking PHI 2010 of my own free will. (C4)

    C6: Therefore, if determinism if true, then I'm not taking PHI 2010 of my free will. (C1,C5)

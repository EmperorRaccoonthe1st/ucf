# Intro To Philosophy
The Paradox of Free Will

## The Paradox of Free Will

    FW1: We have free will.

    FW2: If Determinism is true, then we have no Free Will. 

    FW3: If determinism is false, then we have no Free Will.

    Why is this a paradox? F1 to F3 are:
        Jointly Inconsistent and Individually Plausible

        - Why is F1 Plausible?
            It seems that way perceptually to a individual.
       
        - Why is F2 Plausible?
            The Consequence Argument
       
        - Why is F3 Plausible?
            The Randomness Argument
   
    Paradox:
        Set of propositions that are:
            - (i) Individually plausible
                but
            - (ii) jointly inconsistent

## Opinions on the Free Will Debate

    1. Incompatibilism:
        Determinism is true, then we have no free will (FW2 is true)

        1a: Libertarian Incompatibilism - FW2 is true, FW1 is true, and FW3 is false.

        1b: Hard Incompatibilism - FW2 is true and FW1 is false

    2. Compatibilism:
        Determinism's truth is compatible with free will (FW2 is false)

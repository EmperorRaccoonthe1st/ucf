# Intro to Philosophy
Hard Incompatibilism 

## The Argument from Bad Consequences

    BC1: Belief in hard incompatibilism has bad consequences.

    BC2: We should not hold beliefs that have bad consequences.

    BC3: Therefore, we should not believe hard incompatibilism.

### Defense of BC1

    If hard incompatibilism is true, then no one is morally responsible for their actions.

    The belief that no one is morally responsible leads to additional "dangerous" ideas:

    - The belief that nothing is morally wrong.

    - The belief that criminal punishment is unjustifiable.

    - The belief that there are no genuine personal successes.


## Punishment w/o Moral Responsibility

    Theory of Punishment gives a  general justification for punishing wrongdoers:
        
        - Retributivist Theory:
            Criminal punishment is justified because criminals deserve to be punished for their crimes.

        - Moral Education:
            "Criminal punishment educates criminals morally"

        - Deterrence Theory:
            "Punishing criminals is justified b/c it deters future crime"

        - Contagion Theory:
            Criminal punishment is justified because like quarantine, it prevents the criminal from further harming society.

If I had received the Ring of Gyges my behavior would be altered, but only to the extent of minor lawlessness. 
Such a powerful artifact would only serve to tempt those who hold it, no matter their motivations. 
Whether the bearer desires to help others or to harm them the ring represents a supernatural method to do so. 
It would tempt them continuously to utilize the ring for their benefit, as the power of invisibility would be too great to pass up in many situations.
While the ring would cause small deviations in the behavior of its bearer it would not radically change how they think and act.
A mentally stable person would not decide on a whim to under if they suddenly gained the ability to turn invisible.
Another major shift in environment or mental state would be necessary for them to deviate in y behavior. 
Essentially the ring magnifies the preexisting motivations of those who hold it through their newly acquired abilities. 
These abilities do not in themselves cause major behavioral changes. 
To answer the question of how my behavior would change if I put on the Ring of Gyges: yes it would change, as everyone's would in some small way, but not in any meaningful manner. 
As I am not a murderer nor other sort of dangerous criminal I would not become one once donning the ring.
I would continue to live my life as I currently do, occasionally  attempting to use it to benefit myself when possible, but never in a manner that I wouldn't attempt currently.
The important lesson that can be learned from this thought experiment is one of self-reflection.
It is important to take a step back and attempt to understand oneself as a whole.
This experiment and others like it provide an opportunity to evaluate one's motivations in life in a manner quite different from their usual self reflections.

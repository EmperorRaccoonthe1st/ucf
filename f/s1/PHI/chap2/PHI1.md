# Good Life I

##  Psychological Egoism

> -  Two ways of desiring something:
>        - 1: For the sake of obtaining something _else_.
>           - ex: Money or rare materials
>        - 2: For their _own_ sake.
>           - ex: Pure pleasure, Drugs, Sex
>
> **Psychological Egoism** (PE): "....the only thing anyone is capable
> of desiring or pursuing ultimately (as an end in itself) is his own
> self-interest."

## The Republic II

>   **Socrates's Thesis**:
>    - "We are capable of desiring justice, or morality, for its own sake."
>
>   **Ring of Gyges** story
>
>    -   What happens when Gyges of Lydia puts on the ring?
>         - He became the messenger to the King in order to be allowed into the palace.
>         Once there he seduced the Queen and took control of the kingdom. 
>
>    -   How does Gyges' behavior change?
>         - Gyges decides to act only in his self interest, and give in to his true desires.
>
>    -   What does this change reveal about his true desires and motives?
>         - This reveals that all people truly only care about themselves. That they only act
           out of personal benefit from every interaction.

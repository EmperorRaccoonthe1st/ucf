#  What is Hedonism?
>   - Two kinds of goods:
>
>     - **Instrumental good**:
>        - Something that is good in and of itself or in its own right.
>     - **Intrinsic good**: 
>        - Something that derives its goodness from some other good thing.
>
>    - Hedonistic Theory of Value:
>        - *(i)* pleasure is the only intrinsic good,
>            and
>        - *(ii)* pain is the only intrinsic bad.
>    
>   -  **Hedonism**:
>        - The good life is the life of maximal net pleasure.
>        - **Net Pleasure**: 
>            - The total amount of pleasure minus the total amount of pain across a life.

# Epicurean Hedonism
>    - Two different ways of pursuing the life of maximal net pleasure:
>
>        - _The profligate lifestyle_:
>            - Engage in a short list of immediate desires without thought of the future.
>
>        - _The prudent lifestyle_:
>            - Engage in a life where net pleasure is maximised through careful and measured indulgence.
>
>    -  Q: Which of these lifestyles is most likely to achieve maximal net
> pleasure? Why?
>        - The prudent lifestyle is most likely to achieve maximal net pleasure due to the manner in which 
>         pleasure is acquired. It is obtained in such a way that pleasure is maximised to ones greatest abilities.
    
## The Epicurean Theory of Desire
>     (1) *Natural* desires =  Desires that originate from human nature.
>        - (1a) *Necessary* for happiness, or health,
>                or one's life.
>        - (1b) *Not necessary* for happiness, or health,
>                or life.
>     (2) *Vain* desires = Irrational desires only developed through socialization

## Epicurus on Death

### **Death is Not Bad** (NB)
>     - (NB1) It is rational for me to fear x only if x will be bad for me.
>     - (NB2) X can be good or bad for me _only if_ I can experience it.
>     - (NB3) I cannot experience being dead.
>     - (NB4) Therefore, being dead is not good nor bad for me.
>     - (NB5) Therefore, it is not rational for me to fear being dead. (1, 4)

### Defense of NB3
>     - (3.1) I can experience x only if I exist when it happens.
>     - (3.2) I will not exist when I'm dead.
>     - (3.3) Therefore, I will not be able to experience being dead.

## The Experience Machine
> - What is the **experience machine**:
>    - A machine capable of giving you an experience of series of experiences.

### **Experience Machine Argument** (EM)
>    - (EM1) If hedonism is true, then it would be good for me to plug into the
>    experience machine.
>    - (EM2) It would _not_ be good for me to plug into the EM.
>    - (EM3) Therefore, hedonism is false.
>       - Defense of EM1
>           - You would be able to live the best life possible, which is good.
>       - Defense of EM2
>           - Freedom, Moral Virtue, Knowledge, Love, and Meaning.
